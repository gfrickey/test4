May 19, 2012 - SACistan name first honorary Bo

Lifeguard Bo - Bill Artwohl


Click on Picture for Larger View

We are writing about Bill Artwohl a Cactus Pool lifeguard who just retired and wish to acknowledge his commitment to Scottsdale pools and to the patrons of the Cactus Pool. He is passionate about our safety, cleanliness of the pool and the grounds, and connecting with the pool patrons. Many of our Masters swimmers have been swimming at Cactus for over 20 years and we developed longstanding friendships with Bill and other employees. But it is more than that ... his commitment and caring is contagious and it rubs off on all of us.

Every once in a while when we are not following that black line on the bottom of the pool or playing water polo on Fridays we get a little philosophical and think things like ... do swim teams just happen because there is a pool? Well we all know that's not how it works ... it takes passionate people who care about such things and who make the effort to connect with others; from passionate swimmers and coaches, to caring city employees, and dedicated city administrators, it takes a team.

Bill Artwohl is a dedicated city employee, passionate about safety, the pool and the people who swim there. A quick example; he was the first pool employee that was encouraging and supportive of a fledgling water polo team that blossomed into a regular Friday morning event attracting several dozen players over the last eight years to Cactus pool. Last Friday (May 18th) our masters swim group and polo team with over thirty people present at 5:45AM held a special presentation to acknowledge Bill and gave him a water polo cap that he wears (we think proudly) signed by all of our swim team. He left a positive impression on everyone who knew him and helped make all our time at the pool more enjoyable ... his caring about Cactus pool made us care about Cactus in a personal way.

October 9, 2010 - SACistan swimmers complete 1500 yard fly set
Scottsdale, AZ, October 9.

Submitted by Roving Reporter: Pole Boy

It was a beautiful fall morning at the Cactus Pool Aquatic center in Scottsdale, Arizona. At 7:00am a small group of swimmers arrived at the outdoor pool with the pleasant morning temps in the mid 60's. Water temp was estimated to be about 83 degrees.

This was no ordinary Saturday morning workout for this small band of renegades as they prepared for the fifth annual "Hour of Power" swim. The only question in everyone's mind was, "will I be able to complete the swim this year?".

At 7:16 after the group had completed a light warm-up, the "Hour of Power" began.

It might be helpful to explain what the "Hour of Power" is. It is a pretty simple single set that takes exactly one hour to complete.

60 x 25yds Fly on a 1:00 interval...and each 25 is a no breather.

After each group of 10 swims, one of the participants jumps up on the blocks to sing their college, high school, or elementary school fight song before the group begins the next swim. Since 4 or 5 of the 7 swimmers participating in this year's event were in their mid 50's remembering a school fight song is sometimes a big challenge as well. One swimmer who grew up in Illinois could only remember the fight song for the Chicago Bears so he sang that.

The singing and banter that takes place between each swim is an important part of the tradition and a good laugh enjoyed by the group can have a negative impact on a person's oxygen debt going into the next swim.

Another tradition for the Hour of Power is that most of the swimmers who participate prefer to go by their nick names such as Bo-Peep, 612, Deceptive, Dave Bo, Rico Suave, or The King. This reporter was not able to determine the real names of any of the swimmers in this year's event.

After the completion of the Hour of Power, by all 7 swimmers who started the set, a few of the participants decided to top off the workout with a 50 underwater swim.

Then it was time for handshakes all around, a quick shave and a shower, then off on their separate ways for another beautiful Arizona weekend.

The Official History of SACistan
Once upon a time, some old, but highly  trained athletes (swimmers) joined together to train at the Cactus Park Aquatic Center in Scottsdale, AZ with the Scottsdale Aquatic Club (SAC). All of this was going along fine, till one day, one of the swimmers suggested that after practice it would be a good idea to go to a local bagel eatery for some bagels. You see, one of the things about being a swimmer is that you really don't need to know the people that you swim with all that well. You are underwater most of the time, so usually a quick hello and "how ya doin'" will suffice. However, as these encounters at the bagel eatery became more popular, it became necessary to actually get to know some of the other swimmers - learn of their families, their occupations, and even their views and opinions.

As this is during the age of the Internet, naturally, some of the swimmers began e-mailing little tidbits to one another. One day, an email surfaced about a group that was trying to develop a new nation within the United States to promote greater freedom. Well, this idea seemed ok, but why not just have our own little country for old, highly trained swimmers? Since the USA was engaged in war in Afghanistan at the time, the logical name of the new country became SACistan.

Early founding ideas were - complete freedom - no rules, no bureaucracy, etc. But, of course, as the small country grew, and more old, highly trained swimmers became involved, quickly rules and bureaucracy began to develop. The more mature swimmers in the new country took it upon themselves to establish a constitution without the normal checks and balances of a republican government whereby all authority was retained by the King as head of the Executive Branch using Cabinet Ministers to organize and deliver on important stuff. One of the Ministers issued his proclamations in the form of a great Book. Another quickly took control of a vital component of the new nation and became the SACistan Beermeister. In fact, beer has literally been a key ingredient in the development of SACistan.

Residents of this small country learned that Corona does come in a can. With this information, a "beer relay" was proposed at the pool. But then, the heavy-handed Beermeister (who is a native of Canada) proclaimed that Molson's was the official beer of SACistan. This proclamation did not preclude the consumption of other brands of beer, but certainly did make an important statement to the citizenry.

Soon, some of the citizens even got a bit carried away - thinking of themselves as "reborn swimmers" ............yeah! swimmers who have been to the Mountain Top (Thank You Martin) ...swimmers standing on the starting blocks of a brand New Age........swimmers who have found the courage, the heart, the character to swim in the pool of uncertainty...........swimmers who swim with a new Torch of Hope...... hope not about medals and trophies and championships.....but about holding up the promise of a new vision, a new land...... a new pool where competitors and water aerobic ladies can one day frolic side-by side (well, maybe with Ken in the lane in-between)......where triathletes and retirees can circle swim in camaraderie and brotherhood....where we all can soap and shower together without fear or reprisal (ooooops forget about that one!) and where a case of Molson will be available at the end of every workout for all members of the proud state of Sacistan

The citizens knew that their small country had truly arrived with the introduction of its very own website www.SACistan.com.
